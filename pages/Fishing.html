ABOUT FISHING

ABOUT FISHING STUDIES:

Fisheries are social, biological, and geographic entities dedicated to the production of fish for human use. The science comprises studying freshwater, marine, and aquatic systems which involve humans.

It studies factors affecting capture and stock sustainability. You can start your career in fishing that will enable you to pursue your quest for excellence in aquaculture.
IS FISHERIES STUDIES RIGHT FOR ME?

A background in biology is ideal, as this field requires developing a complex understanding of aquatic animal behavior and their relationship with the natural environment. It also requires constant communication with internal teams, field staff, and the supply chain networks, which means you would need strong communication and team-building skills.
AFTER ADVANCED LEVEL, WHAT NEXT?

Students may further their studies in any of the following fields at a tertiary educational institution of their choice:
- Animal Production - Agriculture
- Veterinary Medicine - Agronomy
- Micro Biology - Natural Sciences
CAREER PATHWAYS FOR FISHERY STUDIES GRADUATES:

Graduates can occupy the following positions at both government or private organizations:
- Fisheries Officer - Fishery Observer
- Fishery Manager - Fisheries Development Officer
- Fishery Technician - Agro-Pastoral Advisor
